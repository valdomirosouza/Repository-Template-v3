# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in our
project and community a welcoming, respectful, and inclusive experience for everyone,
regardless of background or identity. We are committed to acting in ways that
contribute to an open, collaborative, and professional environment.

---

## Our Standards

We expect all participants to demonstrate the following behaviours:

- **Respectful communication** — engage constructively, assume good faith, and
  keep discussions focused on ideas rather than individuals
- **Welcoming collaboration** — actively support new contributors and value
  perspectives that differ from your own
- **Constructive feedback** — give and receive criticism professionally, focusing
  on the work rather than the person
- **Accountability** — accept responsibility for mistakes, apologise when
  appropriate, and treat every incident as a learning opportunity
- **Community focus** — prioritise what is best for the project and the broader
  community over personal interests

Behaviour that undermines these standards is subject to enforcement action.
If you are unsure whether a specific behaviour is appropriate, contact the
maintainers at conduct@\<org-domain\> before acting.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.
They have the authority to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki
edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct,
and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — including the repository,
issue tracker, pull requests, discussion forums, and any official communication
channels. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project
in public spaces, such as conferences, meetups, or social media.

---

## Enforcement

Conduct issues should be reported to the project maintainers at:

**conduct@\<org-domain\>**

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with confidentiality.
The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed without explicit consent.
Responses are proportionate to the nature and severity of the reported issue.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent consequences as determined by the project leadership.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will apply the following response tiers, determined by the nature
and severity of the reported behaviour:

1. **Correction** — a private written clarification and reminder of expected standards
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with defined consequences for continued behaviour
3. **Temporary suspension** — a temporary restriction from participation in project spaces
4. **Permanent removal** — permanent exclusion from all project spaces
